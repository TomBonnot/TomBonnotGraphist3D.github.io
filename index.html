About Me

French student in video games at Master MAJIC in Cannes. I have a degree in computer science and I was interrested in programming but now, I have discovered 3D graphics and I'm focusing on it. I find it really interresting, the only idea of the limits and horizon of it are incredible and amazing and it's inspiring. About video games, I'm a big player myself.
I love to analyze the different gameplay, to see what's working or not and how it works. I love to see the video game industry evolve and increase in size every year, starting from scratch, as a subculture, disregarded by the majority, to an industry weighing billions of dollars, used all around the globe, and requiring a new field of science to work at its best.
I want to work in video games, from the 3D modeling to the animation and integration. I want to create my own objects and maybe even characters. Trough all my years of study I had to work on a various projects so I can make myself fit really fast and well into new groups and work with people. I'm very open-minded and ready to help anyone with any situation.

We created a game this weekend on the theme of "How do you see your Home", and we got an idea.
What is home for us ? At first it's a place where we will feel secure, nott threatened by anything, justt being here and taking good time. But, in our mind if we stay too much long into our home, it could be very dangerous and will close ourselves to the World. We discussed for the first 2 hours of this GGJ (Global Game Jam) and then we started with an idea : Rubik'scare.
It's a rogue like game on the theme of a rubikscube, and we wanted to use a isometric view and a cell shading effect on Unity. We started building levele design, game design, rules of games, how we can play etc... And then we have Cubal, a big colored cube into a colored world, who need to go find his relatives in a dangerous world to build up his home, a rubikscube. He will unlock skills along his path and you will have to help him building his home.
In this game there is actually 5 primary levels, and then 4 levels by skill (they are 4). 5 Halls, 4 levels by skill, why 4 ? It's because, the first Hall you'll complete, it'll be raw, then you unlock a skill. After this, you'll do another Hall, but you have a new skill, then, on of the relatives on this Hall will be places somewhere else to put a level about this skill.
Here you can find the game and everything you need.
